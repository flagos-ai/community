# Frequently Asked Questions

## Contribution

### Q: I don't know where to start contributing?
Start by finding the SIG for the module you're interested in from the [SIG Overview](../sigs/README.md), attend a meeting, and look for issues tagged `good first issue` in that SIG.

### Q: How long until I get a response after submitting a PR?
A SIG Approver will provide initial feedback within 1 week. During the bootstrap phase (before a SIG is fully operational), this may extend to 2 weeks. If no response is received within that time, note this in a PR comment and post in [GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/FlagOS-AI/community/discussions) for help.

### Q: Can I participate in multiple SIGs at once?
Yes. However, we recommend building sufficient contribution experience in one SIG first.

## Governance

### Q: What's the difference between the TSC and a SIG Chair?
The TSC is responsible for cross-SIG decisions and overall project direction. A SIG Chair is responsible for running a single SIG's meetings, managing members, and liaising with the TSC. See [GOVERNANCE.md](../GOVERNANCE.md) for details.

### Q: How do I create a new SIG?
Find ≥1 Chair candidate + ≥1 Tech Lead + ≥3 initial members, and submit a Charter draft to the TSC. See [sigs/README.md](../sigs/README.md) for details.

### Q: When will the OWNERS files be populated?
They will be populated after the TSC confirms Chair candidates in its first meeting. Do not put TBD in OWNERS — leave the position blank if no one is identified.

## FEP

### Q: What if my FEP is rejected?
Rejected FEPs must still be merged as a PR to preserve the decision record. You may revise and resubmit based on the feedback.

### Q: How are cross-SIG FEPs approved?
For FEPs involving multiple SIGs, an Approver from each relevant SIG must approve. The home SIG Chair coordinates.

## Chip

### Q: I'm a chip vendor — how do I get FlagOS to support our chip?
See the [Chip Vendor Onboarding Guide](chip-vendor-guide.md) and submit a Chip Support Request.

### Q: What are the specifications for chip CI resources?
See sig-chip's CI tier requirements. At minimum, the runner must be able to compile and run the FlagOS basic test suite.
